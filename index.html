Kyoto 京都
The spiritual home of matcha. Uji's cool mists and shaded gardens have produced Japan's most revered ceremonial teas for over eight centuries.
Shizuoka 静岡
Japan's largest tea-growing prefecture. Rolling hillsides beneath Mt. Fuji produce refined sencha, culinary matcha, and aromatic black teas.
Kagoshima 鹿児島
Southern Kyushu's warm climate yields Japan's earliest spring harvests — bright, grassy senchas and honeyed wakoucha black teas.

About Sakura Leaf

From tea garden to European table, without compromise.

We operate as a direct-source merchant between specialist Japanese producers and European importers, café groups, confectioners and specialty retailers. Every tea is selected personally each season, tested to EU pesticide-residue and heavy-metal standards, and shipped with full documentation.


Our scale is intentionally small. Matcha B, for example, is capped at 100kg per season because we refuse to blend it down. It is the tea we like best.

Single-origin Sourcing
Every lot traceable to Kyoto, Shizuoka, or Kagoshima — no anonymous blends, no repackaged bulk.
EU Regulation Compliant
All products meet European Union food safety, pesticide-residue and labelling regulations.
